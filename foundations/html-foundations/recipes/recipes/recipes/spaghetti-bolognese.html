Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

Everyone needs a great everyday Spaghetti Bolognese recipe, and this is mine! The Bolognese Sauce is rich, thick and has beautiful depth of flavour. Itâ€™s perfect for a quick midweek meal but even better if you can simmer it for a couple of hours! Serve it over pasta, stuff into jacket potatoes, make an epic Lasagna or Baked Spaghetti Pie!

Ingredients

Serves 2

  • 0.6 tbsp olive oil
  • 0.8 garlic cloves , minced
  • 0.4 onion , finely chopped (brown, yellow or white)
  • 0.4 lb / 200g combined beef and pork mince
  • 0.2 cup (50 ml) dry red wine
  • 0.8 beef bouillon cubes
  • 320g / 11.2 oz can crushed tomato (or tomato passata)
  • 0.8 tbsp tomato paste
  • 0.8 tsp white sugar, if needed
  • 0.8 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 0.8 dried bay leaves
  • 0.8 sprigs fresh thyme (or 1/2 tsp dried thyme or oregano)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium high heat. Add onion and garlic, cook for 3 minutes or until light golden and softened.
  2. Turn heat up to high and add beef. Cook, breaking it up as your go, until browned.
  3. Add red wine. Bring to simmer and cook for 1 minute, scraping the bottom of the pot, until the alcohol smell is gone.
  4. Add remaining ingredients except salt and pepper. Stir, bring to a simmer then turn down to medium so it bubbles gently. Cook for 20 - 30 minutes (no lid), adding water if the sauce gets too thick for your taste. Stir occasionally.
  5. Adjust salt and pepper to taste right at the end. Serve over spaghetti - though if you have the time, I recommend tossing the sauce and pasta per steps below.
